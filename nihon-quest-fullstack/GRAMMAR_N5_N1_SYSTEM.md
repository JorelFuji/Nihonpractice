## 🎮 **Three Learning Modes**

### **1. SOV vs SVO Structure** 🔄

**What It Teaches:**
- Japanese is **SOV** (Subject-Object-Verb)
- English is **SVO** (Subject-Verb-Object)
- The verb always comes LAST in Japanese

**Example Sentences:**

```
🇯🇵 私は 寿司を 食べます
📍 I (S) sushi (O) eat (V)
🇺🇸 I eat sushi

🇯🇵 ママ、レックスのパンツ 忘れちゃ ダメよ
📍 [Lex's underwear] [forgetting] [is-no-good]
🇺🇸 Mom, don't forget Lex's underwear!

🇯🇵 学生が 本を 読みます
📍 Student (S) book (O) read (V)
🇺🇸 The student reads a book

🇯🇵 猫が ネズミを 追いかける
📍 Cat (S) mouse (O) chase (V)
🇺🇸 The cat chases the mouse
```

**Key Points:**
- Particles (は, を, が) mark the role of each word
- Word order is flexible, but verb is ALWAYS last
- Listen/read to the end before translating!

---

### **2. Casual vs Polite Registers** 🎭

**What It Teaches:**
- **Casual** - Family, kids, close friends
- **Polite** - Strangers, coworkers, safe default
- **Formal** - Business, official documents, keigo

**Same Meaning, Three Registers:**

```
👶 CASUAL (Kids/Family):
レックスのパンツ、忘れちゃダメよ
Rekkusu no pantsu, wasurecha dame yo
• Drops particles (を)
• Contractions (ちゃ from ては)
• Ending particles (よ)

🙇 POLITE (General Use):
レックスのパンツを忘れないでください
Rekkusu no pantsu o wasurenaide kudasai
• Full particles present
• Polite request form (~ないでください)
• です/ます forms

💼 FORMAL (Business):
レックスの着替えをお忘れなきよう
Rekkusu no kigae o owasure naki you
• Honorific prefix (お~)
• Written/formal constructions
• Changed vocabulary (着替え instead of パンツ)
```

**When to Use Each:**
- **Casual:** Home, texting friends, anime characters
- **Polite:** Shopping, meeting people, work emails
- **Formal:** Job interviews, official letters, news

**Common Mistake:**
Using casual with your boss! Always default to polite until invited to use casual.

## 🔍 **Grammar Concepts Explained**

### **SOV Word Order**

**Japanese builds sentences differently:**

```
English (SVO):
I → eat → sushi
Subject → Verb → Object

Japanese (SOV):
私は → 寿司を → 食べます
Watashi-wa → sushi-o → tabemasu
Subject → Object → Verb
```

**Why It Matters:**
- Can't translate word-by-word from English
- Must wait for the verb at the end
- Particles show relationships, not word order

---

### **Register System**

**Three Levels of Politeness:**

| Aspect | Casual | Polite | Formal |
|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| **Audience** | Family, friends | General public | Business, officials |
| **Verb ending** | Plain form (食べる) | ます-form (食べます) | Honorific (召し上がる) |
| **Particles** | Often dropped | Always present | Always present |
| **Contractions** | Common (ちゃ) | Rare | Never |
| **Example** | 食べた? | 食べましたか | お食べになりましたか |

**Golden Rule:** When in doubt, use polite (です/ます)!
